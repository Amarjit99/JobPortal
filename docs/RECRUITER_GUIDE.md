## üìù Creating Your Employer Account

### Step 1: Register as Recruiter

1. Visit the Job Portal homepage
2. Click **"Sign Up"**
3. Select **"Recruiter/Employer"** as your account type
4. Complete the registration form:
   - **Full Name:** Your name as the hiring manager
   - **Email Address:** Use your company email
   - **Phone Number:** Business contact number
   - **Password:** Create a secure password
   - **Company Name:** Your organization's name

5. Click **"Create Account"**

### Step 2: Verify Your Email

1. Check your email for verification link
2. Click the link to verify your account
3. You'll be redirected to set up your company profile

**Important:** Use a corporate email address to build trust with candidates.

### Step 3: Complete Company Profile

After verification, complete your company profile to start posting jobs.

## üè¢ Setting Up Company Profile

A complete company profile attracts better candidates and builds credibility.

### Company Registration

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí Company Profile**
2. Click **"Register Company"** (first-time setup)
3. Fill in company details:
   - **Company Name:** Official registered name
   - **Description:** Compelling company overview (300-500 words)
   - **Website URL:** Company website
   - **Location:** Headquarters or main office location
   - **Company Size:** Number of employees
   - **Industry:** Primary industry sector
   - **Founded:** Year established

4. Upload **Company Logo**:
   - Format: PNG, JPG
   - Size: Minimum 200x200px
   - Maximum file size: 2MB
   - Use high-quality, professional logo

5. Click **"Save Company"**

### Writing an Effective Company Description

Your company description should include:

**Paragraph 1: Who You Are**
```
TechCorp is a leading software development company specializing in cloud-based 
solutions for enterprise clients. Founded in 2015, we've grown to a team of 
200+ professionals serving clients across North America and Europe.
```

**Paragraph 2: What You Do**
```
We develop innovative SaaS platforms that help businesses streamline their 
operations, improve productivity, and scale efficiently. Our flagship products 
serve over 10,000 companies worldwide.
```

**Paragraph 3: Your Culture & Values**
```
At TechCorp, we foster a culture of innovation, collaboration, and continuous 
learning. We offer competitive benefits, flexible work arrangements, and 
opportunities for professional growth. We're committed to work-life balance 
and creating an inclusive workplace.
```

**Paragraph 4: Why Join Us**
```
Join us to work on cutting-edge technology, collaborate with talented 
professionals, and make a real impact on businesses worldwide. We offer 
competitive salaries, comprehensive benefits, and a supportive work environment.
```

### Updating Company Profile

1. Go to **Dashboard ‚Üí Company Profile**
2. Click **"Edit Company"**
3. Update any information
4. Upload new logo if needed
5. Click **"Save Changes"**

## üì¢ Posting Job Listings

### Creating a New Job Post

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí Post Job**
2. Fill in the job details form:

#### Basic Information
- **Job Title:** Clear, specific title (e.g., "Senior React Developer")
- **Company:** Select your company from dropdown
- **Job Type:** Full-time, Part-time, Contract, or Internship
- **Location:** City and state/country, or "Remote"
- **Number of Positions:** How many people you're hiring

#### Job Details
- **Job Description:** Comprehensive overview of the role
- **Requirements:** List of must-have qualifications
- **Responsibilities:** Day-to-day duties and expectations
- **Salary Range:** Competitive compensation (increases applications by 40%)
- **Experience Level:** Years of experience required (0-2, 3-5, 5-10, 10+)

#### Application Settings
- **Application Deadline:** Last date to accept applications
- **Contact Email:** Where to send inquiries

3. Click **"Preview"** to review your posting
4. Click **"Post Job"** to publish

### Writing Effective Job Descriptions

#### Job Title Best Practices
‚úÖ **Good Examples:**
- "Senior Software Engineer - React/Node.js"
- "Marketing Manager - Digital Campaigns"
- "Sales Representative - B2B SaaS"

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "Rockstar Developer" (overly casual)
- "Guru" or "Ninja" (unprofessional)
- Vague titles like "Tech Person"

#### Job Description Template

```markdown
## About the Role
[2-3 sentences describing the position and its importance to the company]

## What You'll Do
- [Responsibility 1]
- [Responsibility 2]
- [Responsibility 3]
- [Responsibility 4]
- [Responsibility 5]

## What We're Looking For
**Required:**
- [Must-have skill 1]
- [Must-have skill 2]
- [Must-have qualification]

**Preferred:**
- [Nice-to-have skill 1]
- [Nice-to-have skill 2]

## What We Offer
- Competitive salary: $X - $Y
- Health, dental, and vision insurance
- 401(k) matching
- Flexible work arrangements
- Professional development opportunities
- [Other unique benefits]

## About [Company Name]
[1-2 paragraphs about your company, culture, and mission]

## How to Apply
Click "Apply Now" to submit your application. We review applications on a 
rolling basis and will contact qualified candidates within 2 weeks.
```

### Requirements Section Tips

**Be Specific:**
```
‚úÖ "5+ years of React development experience"
‚úÖ "Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field"
‚úÖ "Experience with AWS, Docker, and Kubernetes"

‚ùå "Experience with frontend technologies"
‚ùå "Good education"
‚ùå "Cloud knowledge"
```

**Separate Required vs. Preferred:**
- Required: Must-have qualifications
- Preferred: Nice-to-have skills that make candidates stand out

**Don't Overload Requirements:**
- Keep required skills to 5-7 items
- Add 3-5 preferred skills
- Overly long lists discourage qualified candidates

### Salary Information

**Including salary increases applications by 40%!**

Options:
1. **Exact Range:** "$80,000 - $100,000 per year"
2. **Starting Salary:** "$80,000+ depending on experience"
3. **Competitive:** "Competitive salary based on experience"

**Tip:** Jobs with salary information get 2-3x more applications.

## üìù Managing Job Posts

### Viewing Your Jobs

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí My Jobs**
2. View all your active and closed job postings
3. See key metrics for each:
   - Number of applications
   - Views
   - Days since posted
   - Status (Active/Closed)

### Editing Job Posts

1. Find the job in **My Jobs**
2. Click **"Edit"**
3. Update any information
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

**Note:** Edited jobs are refreshed in search results.

### Deactivating Jobs

When position is filled or no longer needed:

1. Go to **My Jobs**
2. Find the job
3. Click **"Close Job"** or toggle status to "Inactive"
4. Confirm the action

**Note:** Closed jobs no longer appear in searches but you can still view applications.

### Reactivating Jobs

To reopen a closed position:

1. Go to **My Jobs**
2. Find the closed job
3. Click **"Reactivate"**
4. Update information if needed
5. Job goes live again

### Deleting Jobs

**Caution:** This permanently removes the job and all associated applications.

1. Go to **My Jobs**
2. Click **"Delete"** on the job
3. Confirm deletion

**Recommendation:** Close jobs instead of deleting to maintain application history.

## üë• Reviewing Applications

### Accessing Applications

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí Applications**
2. Or click on a job in **My Jobs** and select **"View Applications"**
3. See all candidates who applied

### Application List View

Each application shows:
- Candidate name and photo
- Applied date
- Current status
- Key skills
- Quick actions (Accept, Reject, View Profile)

### Viewing Candidate Profiles

Click on any candidate to see:
- Full profile information
- Work experience
- Education
- Skills
- Resume (download option)
- Contact information

### Filtering Applications

Use filters to find the best candidates:
- **Status:** Pending, Accepted, Rejected
- **Date Applied:** Newest first, oldest first
- **Skills Match:** Candidates with specific skills
- **Experience Level:** Years of experience

### Sorting Applications

Sort by:
- Date applied
- Candidate name
- Match percentage (if available)

### Updating Application Status

Change status to organize your hiring pipeline:

**Pending ‚Üí Accepted:**
1. Review the candidate's profile
2. Click **"Accept"** or **"Move to Next Stage"**
3. Candidate receives notification
4. Status changes to "Accepted"

**Pending ‚Üí Rejected:**
1. Review the candidate
2. Click **"Reject"**
3. Optionally add rejection reason (for internal notes)
4. Candidate receives polite rejection notification

### Downloading Resumes

1. Open candidate profile
2. Click **"Download Resume"**
3. Resume downloads as PDF

**Bulk Download:**
- Select multiple candidates
- Click **"Download All Resumes"**
- Resumes download as ZIP file

### Taking Notes

Keep track of your evaluation:

1. Open candidate profile
2. Click **"Add Note"**
3. Write your observations
4. Save note (visible only to you and your team)

**Example Notes:**
```
"Strong technical background, good culture fit. 
Schedule phone screen for next week."

"Impressive portfolio projects. Lacks specific experience 
with our tech stack but shows strong learning ability."
```

## üìÖ Scheduling Interviews

### Interview Scheduling

1. Open candidate profile
2. Click **"Schedule Interview"**
3. Fill in interview details:
   - **Interview Type:** Phone, Video, In-person
   - **Date and Time**
   - **Duration:** 30 min, 1 hour, etc.
   - **Interviewer(s):** Select from your team
   - **Location/Link:** Office address or video call link
   - **Notes:** Any preparation instructions for candidate

4. Click **"Send Interview Invitation"**

### Interview Notifications

- Candidate receives email with interview details
- Calendar invitation attached
- Reminder sent 24 hours before interview
- Both parties can reschedule if needed

### Managing Scheduled Interviews

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí Interviews**
2. View all upcoming and past interviews
3. Filter by:
   - Upcoming interviews
   - Completed interviews
   - Cancelled interviews

### Rescheduling Interviews

1. Find interview in **Interviews** tab
2. Click **"Reschedule"**
3. Select new date/time
4. Candidate receives updated invitation

### Adding Interview Feedback

After the interview:

1. Go to interview details
2. Click **"Add Feedback"**
3. Rate candidate on key criteria
4. Write detailed notes
5. Make hiring recommendation
6. Save feedback

**Feedback Template:**
```
Technical Skills: 4/5
Communication: 5/5
Culture Fit: 4/5
Problem Solving: 4/5

Notes: 
Candidate demonstrated strong technical knowledge and excellent 
communication skills. Asked thoughtful questions about the role 
and team. Would be a great addition to the team.

Recommendation: Move forward to final round
```

- Include salary ranges when possible

- Describe your work environment
- Mention team structure
- Share company values
- Include diversity & inclusion commitment

#### 5. Application Process
- Set clear expectations
- Mention timeline for response
- Explain interview process
- Provide contact information

### Reviewing Applications Efficiently

#### Create a Scoring System
```
Technical Skills: _/5
Experience Match: _/5
Culture Fit: _/5
Communication: _/5

Total Score: _/20

Pass to Next Round: 15+
Maybe: 12-14
No: <12
```

#### Batch Processing
- Review applications in batches (10-15 at a time)
- Set aside dedicated time
- Make quick initial decisions
- Deep dive on promising candidates

#### Response Time
- Review applications within 3-5 business days
- Respond to all candidates (even rejections)
- Update candidates on timeline
- Communicate clearly and professionally

### Interview Best Practices

#### Preparation
- Review candidate profile before interview
- Prepare relevant questions
- Share interview format with candidate
- Test video call technology beforehand

#### Conducting Interviews
- Start on time
- Follow structured format
- Take notes during interview
- Allow time for candidate questions
- Explain next steps at end

#### Follow-Up
- Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- Provide timeline for decision
- Keep candidate updated on status
- Give constructive feedback (if requested)

## üîß Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "I can't post a job"

**Possible Causes:**
- Reached job posting limit for your plan
- Missing required fields
- Company profile incomplete

**Solutions:**
1. Check your current plan limits
2. Complete all required fields (marked with *)
3. Ensure company profile is 100% complete
4. Upgrade plan if at limit
5. Contact support if error persists

#### "Applications aren't showing up"

**Solutions:**
1. Refresh the page
2. Check if you're viewing the correct job
3. Verify job is still active
4. Check spam folder for application notifications
5. Clear browser cache
6. Try different browser

#### "Can't download candidate resumes"

**Solutions:**
1. Check if you have resume credits remaining
2. Ensure candidate uploaded a resume
3. Try right-click ‚Üí "Save As"
4. Check browser pop-up blocker
5. Try different browser
6. Contact support if issue continues

#### "Interview scheduling not working"

**Solutions:**
1. Verify candidate email address is correct
2. Check your email isn't in spam
3. Ensure date/time is in the future
4. Try a different time slot
5. Send manual email as backup
6. Contact support

#### "Analytics not updating"

**Note:** Analytics update every 24 hours

**Solutions:**
1. Wait 24 hours after job posting
2. Refresh analytics page
3. Check date range filters
4. Verify job has received applications/views
5. Clear cache and reload
